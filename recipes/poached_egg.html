Microwave Poached Egg

Image from Pexels.

Home-cooked. Cheap. Easy. Quick.

Ingredients

  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup (120 ml) water
  • a pinch of salt or vinegar (optional, helps the egg white hold)

Tools

  • microwave-safe mug or bowl
  • slotted spoon

Instructions

  1. Pour 1/2 cup of water, or enough amount to fully submerge an egg, into a microwave-safe mug or bowl.
  2. Stir the water briefly in order to help spread out heat and prevent hot spots.
  3. Crack the egg gently into the mug/bowl of water. Make sure they are intact to ensure we're doing poached egg not egg drop soup recipe.
  4. Microwave on MEDIUM power (about 600-700 watts). Start with 45-60 seconds. Carefully stir around egg midway.
  5. Check doness. The egg white should be set, and the yolk should be soft. If it needs more time, microwave in 10-second bursts.
  6. Remove carefully with a slotted spoon. Let it drain for a few seconds, then serve.

Tips

  • Pierce the yolk if microwaving an egg whole - but for poaching, it's submerged in water so it's safer.
  • Microwave ovens vary. Yours might need more or less time so test.
  • If your microwave doesn't let you set power, reduce cooking time instead.
